How it works

Four transparent stages. Every candidate gets a clear, justified outcome.

1

Resume scoring

Each resume is scored against your weighted rubric — skills, experience, domain fit. Recruiters see the reasoning per criterion, not just a number.

2

Async screen

Tier B and C candidates get a short, targeted screen by email — 5-7 questions aimed at the gaps the resume revealed. They answer on their own time.

3

Behavioral signals

Cover letter customization, source channel, response time — combined into a behavioral score that catches strong candidates who undersell themselves on paper.

4

Tier routing & response

Every candidate lands in a tier and gets a tier-appropriate, AI-written response. You see the email before it sends. One click dispatches the whole batch.

Every tier gets a real reply

No more silence. No more template-feeling "we went with another candidate." Every applicant hears back in your voice, in their context, within hours.

Tier A Interview-ready

Top ~5%

Action: Fast-track to a human interview. Email invites them to schedule a time within the next week.

"Hi Sarah — your background in distributed systems and your AWS Solutions Architect cert stood out. We'd love to set up a 30-minute conversation this week. Reply with two or three times that work for you."

Tier B Hold pool

Next ~15%

Action: Hiring manager review next week. Email sets expectations honestly and gives a real timeline.

"Hi Marcus — thanks for applying. Your experience lines up well and you're under active review. Expect to hear back within 5-7 business days. No need to follow up."

Tier C Polite decline

~40%

Action: Decline respectfully, name the specific gap, encourage them.

"Hi Priya — thanks for the time you put into your application. After careful review, we moved forward with candidates whose Kubernetes depth matched this role more closely. We wish you the best — please apply again as you grow."

Tier D Quick decline

~40%

Action: Brief, warm decline. No details needed. Doesn't waste their time or yours.

"Hi Alex — thank you for your interest in the role. We won't be moving forward this time, but we appreciate you taking the time to apply and wish you the best in your search."

Common questions

If you don't see your question, send a chat on USTech.Ninja.

Does it actually send real emails, or is it a demo?
Both. By default the portal runs in dry-run mode — every send is logged and shown in the outbox, but nothing leaves the system. Add a RESUMETOOL_EMAIL_API_KEY (from resend.com) and it'll send real email through Resend with full deliverability tracking.
How do you avoid bias in the AI scoring?
The model sees the resume text and your rubric criteria. It does not see name, photo, age, gender, school prestige, or address. Per-criterion reasoning is exposed so your hiring manager can spot a bad call. We also expose the raw scores so you can audit distribution across demographic groups if you collect that data.
What happens to candidate PII?
PII (email, phone) is stripped from any tailored resume we render. The candidate record in the database keeps contact info because you need to email them — but you can wipe the entire record with one click. We never train a model on your candidates.
Can I customize the tier emails?
Yes. Tier-specific tone, length, and content are all editable per requisition. You can also lock the response template and let AI only fill in candidate-specific details (name, strengths, gaps).
What if I don't have an OpenAI key?
The triage pipeline falls back to a transparent keyword-overlap heuristic. It's not as good as GPT-4, but it produces a defensible A/B/C/D distribution and writes the emails from templates. The portal works end-to-end with no API keys whatsoever — useful for demos, pilots, and air-gapped deployments.
How long does implementation take?
For self-hosted: 30 minutes. git clone, set two env vars, run make seed to see a demo, then upload your real requisition. For the Enterprise plan with ATS integration, our team handles the integration in 1–2 weeks.
